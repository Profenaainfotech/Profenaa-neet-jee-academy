1. Exam Purpose & Conducting Body

JEE Main is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and serves two major purposes: acting as the gateway for admissions into NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs, and functioning as the qualifying exam for JEE Advanced.

JEE Advanced is conducted by the IITs on a rotational basis, aimed exclusively at selecting the top-performing students for admissions into the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs).

Only the top 2.5 lakh rankers from JEE Main are eligible to attempt JEE Advanced, showcasing its elite-level filtering.

2. Difficulty Level

JEE Main Difficulty: Questions are largely NCERT-based with moderate conceptual depth. The exam tests speed, accuracy, and fundamental understanding rather than complex problem-solving.

JEE Advanced Difficulty: Considered one of the toughest undergraduate exams in the world. It demands deep analytical thinking, application of multiple concepts in a single question, and advanced problem-solving ability.

Students preparing for IITs must focus on higher-order thinking and exceptional conceptual clarity.

3. Exam Pattern & Question Type

JEE Main Pattern: Contains predictable question types such as MCQs and numerical-value questions. The marking scheme is straightforward, making exam strategy easier for students.

JEE Advanced Pattern: The pattern changes every year and may include:

  • Single & multi-correct MCQs
  • Matrix match
  • Numerical-type questions
  • Partial marking questions
This unpredictability makes Advanced more challenging and dynamic.

4. Syllabus Comparison

JEE Main Syllabus: Closely aligned with NCERT textbooks of classes 11 & 12 with fewer out-of-scope topics. The focus remains on accuracy, formula application, and speed.

JEE Advanced Syllabus: Includes deeper and wider concepts, especially in Physics and Mathematics. It demands excellent command over topics like mechanics, calculus, organic chemistry mechanisms, and advanced problem patterns.

Students aiming for Advanced must go beyond NCERT and practice application-oriented questions regularly.

Which Exam Is Harder?

JEE Advanced is significantly harder due to:

  • Complex, multi-step questions
  • Unpredictable pattern every year
  • Negative marking for multiple-correct answers
  • Higher conceptual depth
  • A competitive pool consisting of the top 2.5 lakh students from JEE Main

JEE Main focuses more on conceptual clarity, formula-based solutions, and speed-oriented practice.

Conclusion

To excel in JEE Main, students should prioritize NCERT mastery, timed mock tests, and consistent practice of previous-year questions.

For JEE Advanced, aspirants must strengthen problem-solving skills, develop mathematical intuition, practice multi-concept questions, and build deep conceptual understanding.

With the right coaching, consistent revision, and a well-planned strategy, students can crack both exams and achieve their dream of entering India’s top engineering institutes.
